Partition of Palestine (1947)

The UN proposed dividing Palestine into Jewish and Arab states, with Jerusalem as an international zone. While Jewish leaders accepted the plan, Arab leaders rejected it, considering it unfair. The ensuing violence escalated, culminating in the 1948 Arab-Israeli War, during which Israel declared independence, and hundreds of thousands of Palestinians became refugees.

Territorial Occupations (1967)

The Six-Day War of 1967 saw Israel capture the West Bank, Gaza Strip, East Jerusalem, and other territories. These areas remain central to the conflict, with Palestinians demanding an independent state on these lands and Israel asserting claims, particularly to East Jerusalem and parts of the West Bank.

Settlements in the West Bank

Israeli settlements in the West Bank, considered illegal under international law, have exacerbated tensions. These settlements are seen by Palestinians as encroachments on their land and obstacles to peace. Israel, however, views them as part of its historical and security needs.

Blockade and Violence in Gaza

Since Hamas took control of Gaza in 2007, Israel has imposed a blockade, citing security concerns. This has led to periodic violence, with both sides suffering casualties. Palestinians in Gaza face severe humanitarian conditions, fueling anger and furthering cycles of violence.
